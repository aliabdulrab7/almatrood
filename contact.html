Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the initial consultation really free?+
Yes, absolutely. Your first consultation with one of our attorneys is completely free and carries no obligation. We believe everyone deserves initial legal guidance without financial barriers.
How quickly can I get an appointment?+
For standard consultations, we typically schedule within 2–3 business days. For urgent matters — particularly criminal defense — we can often arrange a same-day consultation. Call us directly for emergencies.
Is everything I share kept confidential?+
Yes. All communications between you and Al Matrood Law Firm are protected by attorney-client privilege and strict professional confidentiality obligations. Nothing you share will ever be disclosed without your consent.
Do you offer consultations in English?+
Yes. We are fully bilingual in Arabic and English. All consultations, documents, and correspondence can be conducted entirely in English for our international clients.
Can I consult by phone or video call?+
Absolutely. We offer consultations by phone and secure video call for clients who cannot visit our Riyadh office, including clients based outside Saudi Arabia. Simply select your preferred format when submitting the form above.
What documents should I bring to my consultation?+
Bring any documents directly relevant to your matter — contracts, correspondence, court notices, identification, etc. Don't worry if you don't have everything; your attorney will advise you on what is needed after the initial meeting.
How do you charge for your services?+
Our fee structures vary by matter type: fixed fees for defined projects (contract drafting, company formation), hourly rates for complex advisory work, and monthly retainers for ongoing corporate clients. All fees are agreed upfront with full transparency.
Do you handle cases outside Riyadh?+
Yes. While our main office is in Riyadh, we represent clients with matters in courts across Saudi Arabia — including Jeddah, Dammam, Mecca, and Medina — and handle international matters from our Riyadh base.
